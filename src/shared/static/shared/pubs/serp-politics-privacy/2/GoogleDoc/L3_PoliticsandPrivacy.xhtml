Politics and Privacy: What do we need to know about a candidate?

Sam Ellis was a strong supporter of one candidate for President. He agreed with all her stances on the issues. He planned to vote for her in the election. Then, one morning, Sam read in the newspaper that his candidate had once been caught with marijuana, back when the candidate was in high school, over 25 years earlier. Sam was dismayed and now wondered if this political candidate still deserved his vote.

During campaigns, we learn a lot about political candidates. Some of this information is unsavory. Some candidates for political office have abused alcohol or drugs, some have cheated on their spouses or stolen money.
