Teen Smoking: Who is Responsible?

Cigarette smoking causes almost one in five deaths in the U.S. each year. Smoking is linked with cancer, heart disease, and lung disease. It is recognized as addictive. Once people start, they have trouble stopping. It can be easy to tell if someone smokes. Smokers often exhibit signs like coughing, low energy, breathing difficulties, and poor circulation. These problems get worse over time. Doctors believe it is inevitable that smoking will someday lead to an accumulation of health problems.

So why do people smoke? Some say smoking keeps them calm. Others think it makes them look cool. Today, teen smoking rates are lower than in the past. Still, 23% of high school students smoke. Eight percent of middle school students smoke. Many people think tobacco companies should follow tougher guidelines about selling to teens.

Companies use pleasing advertisements and other ways to manipulate kids to smoke. For instance, one brand made flavored cigarettes and advertised them with fun, tropical images. Another brand advertised with hip-hop themes. Critics argue that these actions contradict statements that tobacco companies are trying to stop kids from smoking.
