Teen Smoking: Who is Responsible?

The effects of cigarette smoking currently cause nearly one in five deaths in the U.S. each year. Smoking is associated with health conditions such as cancer, heart disease, and lung disease. In addition, smoking is now widely conceded to be addictive. Once people start smoking, they have trouble stopping because of the addictive qualities of smoking. Oftentimes you can easily discern if someone is a smoker or not. Smokers often exhibit symptoms such as coughing, low energy, respiratory difficulties, and poor circulation. These health problems often build and get progressively worse over time. Doctors believe it is inevitable that smoking will ultimately lead to an accumulation of health concerns.

So why do people smoke? Some say smoking keeps them calm. Others think it makes them look cool. Teen smoking rates are lower than they have been in the past. Nevertheless, currently 23% of high school students and 8% of middle school students smoke. Many people think tobacco companies should follow stricter guidelines about merchandising to youth and teens.

Companies use captivating advertisements and other techniques to manipulate kids into purchasing and consuming cigarettes. For instance, one brand developed flavored cigarettes and championed them with fun, tropical images. Another brand advertised with hip-hop themes. Critics contend that these actions contradict assertions by tobacco companies that they are trying to prevent kids from smoking.
