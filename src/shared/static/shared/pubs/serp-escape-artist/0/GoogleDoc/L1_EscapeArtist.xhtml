Escape Artist

What has eight arms, two eyes, and a beak? You guessed it. The octopus. It doesn’t have a nose, ears, or fingers, but each arm is covered with a double row of white cups  called suckers . It uses these suckers to explore its world. Each sucker moves by itself like the way we wiggle our toes or fingers. And a sucker not only holds onto objects, it tastes them. An octopus has a beak like a bird, and a tongue—called a radula —that’s covered with very sharp teeth. These teeth can cut through crab and snail shells like a saw . An octopus will often carry a crab back to its home for dinner. Then, after it’s finished eating, the octopus will deposit the shells just outside its home. Very tidy!

The octopus is the great shape-shifter of the sea. It can make itself big like an umbrella. It can make itself long and thin like a piece of rope. It can squeeze through cracks under a rock. It can flatten itself against the walls of a cave. The octopus is also a master at  hiding . Its skin can change color very quickly . This is a way to protect itself from sharks and other dangerous animals.

When it comes to brains, the octopus goes to the head of the class. It is the smartest of all animals without bones. It can find its way through a maze or figure out how to unscrew the lid of a jar and remove the tasty fish that’s inside. The octopus is a very curious animal  and can even die of boredom if kept in a tank with no world to explore and nothing to interest it.

It can be sneaky, too. Scientists at an aquarium  were confused by an unexplained disappearing act. Every day they’d place new fish in a tank, but when they returned in the morning, the fish would be gone. Finally, they set up a video camera to see who was stealing the fish. They watched in surprise as an octopus in a tank across the room waited for the museum to close, then squeezed its way out of its tank, slid across the floor, slipped into the fish tank, and ate all the fish. Then it crawled back into its own tank and took a nap!
