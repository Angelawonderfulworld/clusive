Escape Artist

What has eight arms, two eyes, and a beak? You guessed it. The octopus. While it does not have a nose, ears, or fingers, each of the octopus’s arms is covered with a double row of white suction cups called suckers . These suckers are sensory organs that an octopus uses to explore its world. Each individual sucker moves by itself, similar to the way humans wiggle their toes or fingers. Even more interesting, each sucker not only grips  objects, it tastes them as well. An octopus has a beak like a bird, and a tongue—called a radula —that’s covered with razor-sharp teeth, and that enable an octopus to cut through crab and snail shells like a buzzsaw . An octopus will often carry a crab back to its den for dinner. Then, after it’s finished eating, the octopus will deposit the empty shells just outside its den. Very tidy!

​An octopus can hide is small places and change color to blend into its surroundings.

The octopus is the great shape-shifter of the sea. It can expand and become wide like an umbrella, or elongate and become thin like a piece of rope. An octopus can squeeze through cracks under a rock or flatten itself against the walls of a cave. Not only can the octopus modify its shape whenever it wants, it can change its appearance, too. Some people have called the octopus a master of disguise . Its skin can change color instantaneously , acting as a camouflage  to protect it from sharks and other predators .

When it comes to brains, the octopus goes to the head of the class. It is the smartest of all invertebrates. It can find its way through a maze or figure out how to unscrew the lid of a jar and remove the tasty fish that’s inside. The octopus is a very curious organism  and can actually die of boredom if kept in a tank with no world to explore and nothing to entertain  it.

It can be pretty sneaky, too. Scientists at an aquarium  were baffled  by an inexplicable disappearing act. Every day they’d place new fish in a tank, but when they returned in the morning, the fish would be gone. Finally, they set up a video camera to see who was stealing the fish. They watched in amazement  as an octopus in a tank across the room waited for the museum to close, then squeezed its way out of its tank, slid across the floor, slipped into the fish tank, and consumed all the fish. Then it crawled back into its own tank and took a nap!
