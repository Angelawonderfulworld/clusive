Censorship: Who Should Decide What Young People Read?

Each year, people try to keep some books out of schools and libraries. These people think students should not have access to literature about things they think are improper for them. Many popular books have been forbidden in recent years. Books like To Kill a Mockingbird by Harper Lee have been forbidden. The Giver by Lois Lowry and The Bluest Eye by Toni Morrison are said to be improper. Even Harry Potter has been forbidden.

Some people think that youth should be protected from difficult issues. Racism or violence are bad for kids. These people also think that books can give kids bad ideas. Other people think that youth face tough issues in their lives already. Those issues should be integrated into meaningful stories. That way, young people might find books helpful for understanding their own lives. For example, when a novel includes a problem such as racism, it can help kids dealing with prejudice to feel less alone. It might also help them to see how their actions can affect other people.

Librarians and teachers try to promote positive attitudes toward reading. They like to see students interested in literature. Some librarians and teachers want censored books to be accessible to students. These books are often the most interesting to read. Many American citizens argue that book banning and other types of censorship violate their civil rights.

Should students have the freedom to read anything they choose despite the possible dangers?
