Are green technologies worth the investment?

As Amanda walked through the park, she noticed a big new trash can called a BigBelly. When she looked it up on the web, it turned out to cost $6,000! The BigBelly has a solar-powered compactor inside. It maximizes the amount of trash it holds, while lessening the number of times it needs to be emptied. The BigBelly is one of many “green technologies ” now available. But does it make sense to invest so much money in them? City managers and mayors say that each BigBelly will pay for itself in three years. They only need to be emptied once a week. Traditional trash cans get emptied once or twice a day, at a cost of about $2,000 a year each. The trucks that travel around to empty them use gasoline and dirty the air. But buying a lot of BigBelly cans could punch a big hole in any city’s budget!   Wind turbines and solar energy are types of green technologies. These wind turbines capture the energy of the wind and convert it into electricity.

The BigBelly uses a renewable energy source, the sun. Many other renewable technologies are growing . Some people are putting solar panels on their roofs. Some are switching from electricity produced with coal or gas to solar-powered electricity. Solar-powered cars and planes

are under development. Solar-powered light strips have been put on some rural highways. These light strips run for free and require little upkeep . Investors are developing solar panels that can replace the asphalt on highways.  These panels use solar power to generate heat (so that ice and snow melt immediately), light (so that streetlamps are unnecessary), and extra electricity for nearby towns and cities.

Investment in these solar technologies is expensive. The Department of Transportation gave $100,000 to the solar pavement project, but several million more will be needed just to finish the development. Are these expensive technologies worth it? Should we keep putting money into technologies that may never work  on a large scale? Shouldn’t we conserve public money for things like improving schools and fixing roads? What if we make green technologies but don’t have the money to proceed with using them? How high a price should we pay for green technology?
