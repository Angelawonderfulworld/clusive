Do the Benefits of Renting a Pet Outweigh the Potential Harm it Can Cause the Animals?

Davonte Reese wanted a dog, but his parents didn’t think he was capable of taking care of one. Then they heard about a company that rents pets. Davonte’s parents were pleased that the pet rental company gave them the chance to rent a Dalmatian for a day. Renting a Dalmatian helped them realize that Davonte was ready to own a dog. As a result, Davonte’s parents took him to an animal shelter and adopted a mixed-breed dog named Lucky.

Animal Shelters

Animal shelters are community businesses that house and protect animals. Not only can people adopt pets from animal shelters, when you get a new pet, you can learn about how to care for your pet at animal shelters. In addition, you can learn about laws that protect animals or even discover laws that protect people who have neighbors with noisy pets!

Some people say renting a dog should be prohibited. They believe rental businesses are harmful to dogs; to be happy, they say, a dog needs ongoing contact with one person. People who are against pet rentals give several other reasons to support their position. For example, a dog likes to know its home.

Dogs and people transformed together, so dogs depend on their human “family.” They have some social skills that are similar to those of humans. People acculturated dogs to be socially compatible and live with humans. Therefore, people have a special responsibility to ascertain that dogs have stable homes. Dogs have a special right to a permanent home with ongoing affection and comradeship. Also, who will want rental dogs when they become sick, old, or infirm?

Domesticated Animals

Originally, all animals were wild animals. People began to domesticate animals (or train them) as helpful workers and pets many thousands of years ago. Dogs were helpful for shepherding sheep, alerting people to danger and helping people keep warm. Dogs are descended from or come from wolves. Other animals that were domesticated include sheep, cats, and goats.

Marlena Davis argues against the notion that pet rental has a negative impact on dogs. She has a pet rental business in which she buys mostly purebred dogs, and then rents them for $50 or more a day. A dog in her pet rental business has the opportunity to spend time with a different family every day of the week. Whereas people who own dogs leave them home alone, Marlena says people who rent dogs give the animals a great deal of attention. Also, families like Davonte’s can use pet rentals to make an informed decision about whether owning a dog is right for them, or not. In addition, Marlena contests that her business also creates jobs for her community.
