What is the Purpose of School?

Why do people attend educational institutions? Some individuals think the principal goal of education is imparting knowledge to students; they feel there is precise information that all individuals in school should acquire. For instance, they want kids to be aware of what happened in the Revolutionary War, or the mechanisms involved in food chains, or other essential information. Others interpret the main role of school as one of preparing students to get a good job and earn a living. They are most perturbed about students needing to learn particular skills and competencies, including reading, writing, and arithmetic; skills that are core for joining the workforce.

Some individuals argue that schools should introduce a set of shared values, including liberty and justice. They believe this will help students understand the structure of our democratic government. For example, they feel it is essential for students to understand that while each of the three branches of government has a unique function, the three combine to work together to make sure we all enjoy certain freedoms and live by the same rules.

Some think schools should educate students to analytically think and analyze what they see, hear, and read. They want students to be able to carefully consider different perspectives, to respect and challenge other viewpoints, and to formulate their own opinions about issues that affect them. Although many people say that they want kids to be able to think for themselves, students do not always have the freedom to do so in contemporary classrooms.
